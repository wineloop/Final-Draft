What am I looking at?

Unfortunately, this was the least confusing photo I could find. Essentially, we are seeing the back of the PCB for a single switch. The switch has five holes what have pins going through them in different sizes, colors, and shapes. The pins we care about are the ones going through the golden holes. And these silver pins poking now need to be soldered to create a more secure connection between the switch and pcb. While soldering is not absolutely necessary, you will most likely face feedback issues with certin keys

Brandon Taylor does a solid guide on soldering and makes the process less daunting then it should be. As mentioned in the video, it is key to make sure you are in a ventilated area and consider wearing safety goggles in the event of a freak accident. And finally, make sure to only apply heat on the pad for about 2-4 seconds max to avoid damaging the pcb. Although PCBs are built to withstand immense heat, it is generally not in your best interest to limit test on an expensive piece of tech.
